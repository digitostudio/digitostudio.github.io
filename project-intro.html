IntroHQ

Building bots to help networking professionals connect more efficiently.

  • Web
  • UI
  • UX
  • 2016

Ever wonder how to leverage your Slack team member’s professional network? Well, Intro is building platform that enables Slack teams to do just that. Intro also has a Slack app that makes it easy for teams to connect with each other and search for people in extended networks. Neat, huh?

They hired Christian to redesign their existing marketing website and web app and to help take Intro to the next level.

The Process

Professional Social Networks

Social networks are a dime a dozen, but there are very few professional networks that leverage your team's network. Most popular is Linkedin. We use other platforms such as Twitter and Google Plus to make introductions and find new opportunities, however, more and more we are finding that these networks are loosely connected and often times connections are not very strong.

Personas

First, Christian needed to understand who the target users are in order to best design for them. So we developed personas. Personas helped the team understand what each user cares about most.

Use-cases to User Flows

From the personas we were able to develop use-cases. Each use-case was broken out into 3 to 4 sub use-cases.

These use-cases were then translated from goals to objectives and Christian was then able to visually represent each visually as user-flows.

Wireframes

Following the user-flows exercise, Christian created wireframes to better understand the UI components that made up each step of the user-flow. In this step, there is still plenty of flexibility to rethink the UX and interaction of each component. There's also opportunity to start thinking about the information hierarchy, layout, and overall structure of the user interface.

Visual Design

Once the wireframes were approved, Christian then focused on creating high-fidelity design mockups in Sketch. Here's where ideas begin to solidify and the user interface starts to take shape.

Interactive Prototypes

Next, it was important to the team to get a better feel for the flow, so Christian used inVision to prototype each flow. The team loved being to click through each flow and provide feedback. This step helped test and validate the team’s assumptions.

Bonus Assets

As a bonus, Christian created animated gifs of the prototype and shared them with the team. The GIFs were so well recieved that they decided to use them in the marketing website as informative content for new users.
